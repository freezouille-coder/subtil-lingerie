Watercolor botanical composition for a website background banner, evoking elegant feminine lingerie boutique branding.

Composition: large painterly watercolor florals — magnolia blossoms in CREAM and IVORY petals with deep AUBERGINE centers, peony heads in DEEP BURGUNDY and TERRACOTTA, with delicate stems, leaves, and a few golden champagne accents. The flowers cluster on the LEFT side of the frame, with generous CREAM/IVORY empty space on the RIGHT side (this empty space is critical: it will host overlay text and a form on the website).

Mood: feminine, refined, airy, hand-painted, never busy.
Style: traditional watercolor on cold-pressed paper, visible brush strokes, soft paint bleeds, slight paper grain texture, NOT digital, NOT photographic, NOT cartoon. Reminiscent of fashion illustrator Inslee Fariss or watercolor artist Tara Sweeney.
Color palette: deep aubergine #6B3E5E, BURGUNDY #722F37, TERRACOTTA #C97D5E, champagne gold #C9A961, cream #FAF6F1 background.
Composition: very wide horizontal banner 21:9, flowers anchored left/lower-left, lots of negative space on the right two-thirds.

STRICT CONSTRAINTS — DO NOT VIOLATE:
- NO PASTEL MAUVE, NO LILAC, NO PASTEL PURPLE, NO LAVENDER, NO PINK
- NO figures, NO bodies, NO faces, NO people
- NO text, NO logos, NO writing of any kind
- NO photographic realism — must look like a hand-painted watercolor
- NO heavy saturation, keep it powdery and soft
- The right two-thirds must remain mostly empty cream background for layout overlay
- Use ONLY warm tones: aubergine, burgundy, terracotta, gold, cream — NEVER cool violet/mauve/pink hues
